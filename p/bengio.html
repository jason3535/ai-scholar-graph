Yoshua Bengio

Mila / U. Montreal

Mila 创始人 · 2018 图灵奖

deep-learningnlp
1964年生于巴黎，塞法迪犹太家庭，父母从摩洛哥移民至法国后定居加拿大。兄弟 Samy Bengio 也是 AI 研究者（现在 Apple）。在麦吉尔大学获电气工程学士、计算机科学硕士和博士（1991，导师 Renato De Mori）。在 MIT 跟随 Michael I. Jordan 做博后，也曾在 AT&T 贝尔实验室工作。1993年加入蒙特利尔大学至今。创办 MILA（蒙特利尔学习算法研究所），现为全球最大的学术深度学习实验室。开创了神经概率语言模型（2003）、注意力机制用于机器翻译（2014，与 Bahdanau 合作），并指导了 Ian Goodfellow 发明 GAN（2014）。2016年联合创办 Element AI（2020年被 ServiceNow 收购）。AI 安全与治理的领军人物。2018图灵奖。2017年获加拿大勋章。
Born 1964 in Paris, France, into a Sephardic Jewish family that emigrated from Morocco to Canada. Brother Samy Bengio is also an AI researcher (now at Apple). Earned BS in Electrical Engineering, MS and PhD in Computer Science all from McGill University (PhD 1991, advisor: Renato De Mori). Postdoc at MIT with Michael I. Jordan and at AT&T Bell Labs. Joined Universite de Montreal as faculty in 1993 and has remained since. Founded MILA (Montreal Institute for Learning Algorithms), now the world's largest academic deep learning lab. Pioneered neural probabilistic language models (2003), attention mechanisms for machine translation (2014, with Bahdanau), and supervised Ian Goodfellow's invention of GANs (2014). Co-founded Element AI (2016, sold to ServiceNow 2020). A leading voice on AI safety and governance. Shared the Turing Award (2018). Named Officer of the Order of Canada (2017).
