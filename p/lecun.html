Yann LeCun

AMI Labs

AMI Labs 创始人 · 2018 图灵奖

deep-learningvision
1960年生于巴黎近郊。获 ESIEE Paris 工程师文凭（1983），巴黎第六大学计算机科学博士（1987）。1987-88年在多伦多大学跟随 Hinton 做博后，这段经历对他影响深远。加入 AT&T 贝尔实验室（1988-1996），在此发明了卷积神经网络（CNN）和 LeNet，用于手写数字识别——最早在银行业实际部署的深度学习系统之一。2003年任 NYU 教授，2012年创办 NYU 数据科学中心。2013年成为 Facebook AI Research (FAIR) 首任主任，后升任 Meta 首席 AI 科学家。倡导自监督学习，对大语言模型推理能力持批评态度。2018图灵奖。2025年11月因 FAIR 预算削减和管理层变动离开 Meta。在巴黎创办 AMI Labs（Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs），尚未正式发布产品即以 35 亿美元估值融资——专注于构建「世界模型」：能理解物理规律、维持持续记忆、规划复杂行为的 AI 系统，而非仅仅预测下一个词。他与 Alexandre LeBrun 共同创办 AMI，在未发布任何产品前即完成 10.3 亿美元种子轮、投前估值 35 亿美元（2026.3.10 公布），总部设在巴黎，并在纽约、蒙特利尔、新加坡设点。
Born 1960 near Paris, France. Earned an engineering degree from ESIEE Paris (1983), then a PhD in Computer Science from Universite Pierre et Marie Curie (1987). Did a postdoc with Geoffrey Hinton at the University of Toronto (1987-88), a pivotal experience. Joined AT&T Bell Labs (1988-1996) where he developed convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and LeNet for handwritten digit recognition -- one of the first practical deep learning systems deployed in banking. Became a professor at NYU (2003), founded the Center for Data Science (2012). Became the first director of Facebook AI Research / FAIR (2013) and rose to Chief AI Scientist at Meta. A vocal advocate for self-supervised learning and frequent critic of large language models' reasoning capabilities. Shared the Turing Award (2018). Left Meta in November 2025 after FAIR budget cuts and leadership changes. Founded Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs (AMI Labs) in Paris, targeting a $3.5B valuation before even launching -- focused on building 'world models': AI systems that understand physics, maintain persistent memory, and plan complex actions rather than simply predicting the next word. He co-founded AMI with Alexandre LeBrun; the startup raised a $1.03B seed at a $3.5B pre-money valuation (announced 10 March 2026) before launching any product, HQ in Paris with offices in New York, Montreal and Singapore.
