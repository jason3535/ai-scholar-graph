1961年生于台北。哥伦比亚大学学士，CMU 博士（语音识别）。历任 Apple、SGI、微软副总裁。创办微软中国研究院。Google 中国总裁（2005-2009）。创办创新工场。2023年创办 01.AI。著有《AI 超级大国》。
Born 1961 in Taipei, Taiwan. Grew up in Tennessee, USA. Earned a BS in Computer Science from Columbia University (1983), then a PhD from Carnegie Mellon University (1988), where his thesis on speaker-independent continuous speech recognition using Hidden Markov Models was named the 'Most Important Innovation' by BusinessWeek. Career at the intersection of industry and China: Apple (1990-1996, led speech recognition), SGI VP, Microsoft VP (founded Microsoft Research China in Beijing, 1998). Became President of Google China (2005-2009), overseeing Google's expansion in China. Founded Sinovation Ventures (2009), one of China's top AI-focused VC firms. In 2023, founded 01.AI to build open-source large language models, quickly reaching unicorn status. Author of 'AI Superpowers: China, Silicon Valley, and the New World Order' (2018), one of the most influential books on AI geopolitics.
