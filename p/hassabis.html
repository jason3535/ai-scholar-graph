1976年生于伦敦，父亲希腊-塞浦路斯裔，母亲新加坡华裔。4岁起的国际象棋神童，13岁达到大师水平（Elo 2300），曾担任英格兰青少年国际象棋队队长。就读于 Barnet 的 Queen Elizabeth's School。17岁加入 Bullfrog Productions，与 Peter Molyneux 联合设计了经典游戏《主题公园》（1994）。在 Lionhead Studios 担任《黑与白》首席 AI 程序员（2001）。创办自己的游戏工作室 Elixir Studios（1998-2005）。后转向神经科学：在剑桥大学女王学院获计算机科学本科（一等荣誉，1997），在伦敦大学学院获认知神经科学博士（2009，导师 Eleanor Maguire，研究海马体中的记忆与想象）。2010年与 Shane Legg 和 Mustafa Suleyman 联合创办 DeepMind。2014年 Google 以约5亿美元收购 DeepMind。领导了 AlphaGo（2016年击败李世石）、AlphaFold（解决蛋白质折叠，2021年 Science 年度突破）和 Gemini。2024年因 AlphaFold 蛋白质结构预测与 John Jumper 共获诺贝尔化学奖。2025 年底 Gemini 3 与爆红的「Nano Banana」图像生成助推 Alphabet 全年股价上涨约 65%，据报道令 OpenAI 拉响「红色警报」。他在 Isomorphic Labs 力争 2026 年底将 AI 设计的候选药物推入临床，并预言一个科学发现的「新文艺复兴」正在到来。
Born 1976 in London; father Greek Cypriot, mother Chinese Singaporean. A chess prodigy from age 4, achieving master standard (Elo 2300) by age 13 and captaining the England junior chess team. Attended Queen Elizabeth's School in Barnet. At 17, joined Bullfrog Productions where he co-designed the classic game Theme Park (1994) with Peter Molyneux. Became lead AI programmer on Black & White at Lionhead Studios (2001). Founded his own game studio Elixir Studios (1998-2005). Then pivoted to neuroscience: earned a PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience from University College London (2009) under Eleanor Maguire, studying memory and imagination in the hippocampus. Read at Queens' College Cambridge for his undergraduate degree (Computer Science, double first, 1997). Co-founded DeepMind in 2010 with Shane Legg and Mustafa Suleyman. Google acquired DeepMind for ~$500M in 2014. Led AlphaGo (defeated Lee Sedol, 2016), AlphaFold (solved protein folding, Science Breakthrough of the Year 2021), and Gemini. Awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry (2024) alongside John Jumper for AlphaFold's protein structure prediction. In late 2025 Google's Gemini 3 and the viral 'Nano Banana' image generator helped lift Alphabet's shares ~65% on the year and reportedly pushed OpenAI to declare a 'code red.' At Isomorphic Labs he aims to move AI-designed drug candidates into clinical trials by the end of 2026, predicting a coming 'new renaissance' of scientific discovery.
